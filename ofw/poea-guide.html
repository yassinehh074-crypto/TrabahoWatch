OFW Resource Guide

DMW / POEA Safe Deployment Guide 2026

Everything you need to know before leaving the Philippines — how to verify your agency, what fees are legal, your rights abroad, and how to avoid illegal recruiters.

OFW in distress abroad? Call the DFA OFW Crisis Hotline: +63 2 8651-9400 or contact the nearest Philippine Embassy or Consulate. DMW Hotline: 1348 (free from landlines).

What is the DMW (formerly POEA)?

The Department of Migrant Workers (DMW) was created by Republic Act 11641 (2022) and absorbed the functions of the former Philippine Overseas Employment Administration (POEA). The DMW is now the primary government agency responsible for:

  • Licensing and regulating private recruitment agencies
  • Processing OFW documentation and contracts
  • Adjudicating complaints against illegal recruiters
  • Running the OWWA (Overseas Workers Welfare Administration)
  • Managing government-to-government (G2G) deployment programs

The official DMW website is dmwph.com. Always use this site to verify agencies and check job orders — never rely on social media posts alone.

Safe Deployment: Step-by-Step

1

Verify your recruitment agency at DMW

Before signing anything, check that your agency has a valid DMW license at dmwph.com or call 1348. A valid license number, expiry date, and authorized representative should all be visible. Unlicensed = illegal recruiter.

2

Review and understand your employment contract

Your contract must specify: position, salary, work hours, rest days, accommodation, food, medical benefits, and contract duration. Never sign a blank contract or one different from what was offered. The DMW must authenticate your contract before departure.

3

Attend the Pre-Departure Orientation Seminar (PDOS)

PDOS is mandatory for all first-time OFWs and required for repeat OFWs going to a new country. It covers your rights, the laws of your destination country, emergency contacts, and remittance options. Held at OWWA offices nationwide — free of charge.

4

Get your OEC (e-OEC / OFW Exit Clearance)

The OEC exempts you from travel tax and terminal fees at the airport. Apply online at ofw.gov.ph or at any DMW / OWWA office. Required for all documented OFWs departing the Philippines.

5

Register with OWWA before departure

OWWA membership (₱550 per 2-year contract) entitles you to benefits including death and disability benefits, repatriation assistance, livelihood programs for returning OFWs, and educational scholarships for your children.

6

Register with the Philippine Embassy on arrival

Once abroad, register with the nearest Philippine Embassy or Consulate. This ensures the embassy can reach you in emergencies, disasters, or conflict situations. Most embassies now offer online registration through the DFA eBayad portal.

Legal Placement Fees — What Agencies Can Charge

Maximum placement fee = 1 month of your contracted salary. Any amount above this is illegal. Never pay before your visa is processed and job order is verified.

Red Flags — Signs of Illegal Recruitment

  • No DMW license — agency cannot show a valid DMW license number
  • Recruits through Facebook / social media only with no physical office address
  • Demands large fees upfront before visa issuance or any documents are processed
  • Offers "too good to be true" salaries for unskilled positions (e.g., ₱200,000/mo domestic helper)
  • Asks you to travel to another country first (e.g., "transit through Dubai then apply for the job")
  • Cannot provide a copy of the job order verified by the DMW
  • Pressures you to leave immediately without proper documentation or PDOS

Your Rights as an OFW Abroad

The Migrant Workers Act (RA 8042 as amended by RA 10022) and the DMW Act (RA 11641) protect all Filipino workers abroad regardless of immigration status.

  • Right to receive your contracted salary on time and in full — no unauthorized deductions
  • Right to keep your own passport — employers cannot confiscate your travel documents
  • Right to rest days — at least one day off per week is standard in most bilateral agreements
  • Right to free repatriation if your employer terminates you without just cause — cost charged to the employer
  • Right to access Philippine Embassy services — legal assistance, temporary shelter, welfare assistance
  • Right to remit money home without employer interference — use BSP-accredited remittance centers

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I still need an OEC if I am a balikbayan?

Balikbayan OFWs (returning to the same employer and country within one year) may be exempt from the OEC. However, first-time OFWs and those changing employers or countries always need an OEC. Check at ofw.gov.ph to confirm your status.

Can I change employers while abroad?

This depends on the laws of your destination country. In Saudi Arabia (2021 reform) and UAE, workers can transfer employers under certain conditions. In Kuwait and Qatar, mobility rules have also improved. Always consult the Philippine Embassy before transferring to avoid legal complications.

What is OWWA and do I need to be a member?

OWWA (Overseas Workers Welfare Administration) is mandatory for all documented OFWs. The membership fee is USD 25 (≈ ₱1,400) per deployment. Benefits include disability/death benefits up to ₱100,000, repatriation, education scholarships (OEPB), and livelihood programs for returning OFWs.

My employer did not pay my salary. What do I do?

First, contact the Philippine Embassy or OWWA Welfare Desk in your country. They can mediate with your employer. If unresolved, the recruitment agency in the Philippines is jointly liable — file a complaint at DMW. Keep copies of your contract, pay slips, and all communications.
