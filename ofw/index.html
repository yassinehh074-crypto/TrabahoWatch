Working Abroad — What You Need to Know

Free PDOS at DMW

The Pre-Departure Orientation Seminar (PDOS) is mandatory, free, and conducted at DMW offices. It covers your rights, destination country laws, and emergency contacts. Never skip it.

OEC Exempts You From Fees

Your Overseas Employment Certificate (OEC) exempts you from travel tax (₱1,620) and terminal fee (₱550) at the airport. Process it at DMW or through the BaM App before departure.

Illegal Recruitment — Red Flags

Beware of recruiters promising unusually high salaries, charging excessive fees, or asking for payment before a verified job offer. Report to DMW hotline 1348.

How to Deploy Safely as an OFW

The official DMW/POEA-approved process

Full DMW Guide →
1

Verify the Agency

Before anything else — check the recruitment agency's license at dmwph.com. Only DMW-accredited agencies can legally deploy OFWs. This single step prevents most illegal recruitment.

2

Review Your Contract

Your POEA Standard Employment Contract must specify: exact salary, work hours, accommodation, airfare, and repatriation terms. Never sign a blank or incomplete contract. Get a copy before departure.

3

Attend PDOS (Free)

The Pre-Departure Orientation Seminar at DMW is free and mandatory. It covers your rights, destination country laws, emergency contacts, and remittance options. Online PDOS is available at pdos.dmwph.gov.ph.

4

Register as OFW

Register at bam.dmwph.gov.ph (Bagong OFW App) or at any DMW office. Your OFW e-Registration and OEC (Overseas Employment Certificate) exempt you from airport fees.

5

Credential Verification

Most Gulf and Asia Pacific countries require apostille or DataFlow credential verification for licensed professionals (nurses, engineers). Start early — DataFlow takes 4–8 weeks. DFA apostille requires original documents.

6

Depart and Register Abroad

Upon arrival, register with the Philippine Embassy or Consulate within 30 days. Keep all your documents in a safe place. Open a Philippine bank account (BDO, BPI, Landbank) for remittance before you leave.
