Our Methodology

How we collect, verify, and publish job information — and how we decide what goes on TrabahoWatch.

Our commitment: TrabahoWatch is an information platform — we do not process job applications and are not a recruitment agency. We publish verified job information so every Filipino can make an informed decision about their career, without paywalls, registration fees, or premium subscriptions.

1. Data Sources

We source job information from the following official and publicly accessible channels:

Government Jobs

  • CSC Job Portal (csc.gov.ph) — The Civil Service Commission's official repository of all government vacancy announcements. Primary source for all plantilla positions.
  • Agency Official Websites — DepEd (depedncr.deped.gov.ph), DOH (doh.gov.ph), PNP (recruitment.pnp.gov.ph), BFP (bfp.gov.ph), NBI (nbi.gov.ph), and respective LGU websites.
  • Philippine Government Official Gazette (officialgazette.gov.ph) — for executive orders, salary standardization laws, and legislative updates.
  • Department of Budget and Management (DBM) — for salary standardization schedules, PERA rates, and bonus guidelines.

Private Sector Jobs

  • Company Career Pages — Direct sourcing from employer websites (careers.concentrix.com, bdo.com.ph/careers, stlukesmedical.com.ph/careers, etc.)
  • JobStreet Philippines (jobstreet.com.ph) — verified employer listings only.
  • Kalibrr Philippines (kalibrr.com) — for tech and professional sector vacancies.
  • LinkedIn Philippines — for professional and corporate sector vacancies.

OFW Jobs

  • DMW (Department of Migrant Workers) job orders database (dmwph.com) — verified job orders from accredited employers only.
  • Overseas Employer Career Pages — kfmc.med.sa, phsa.ca, jicwels.or.jp, and other directly verifiable employer sources.
  • BSP (Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas) — for foreign exchange reference rates used in peso-equivalent salary calculations (bsp.gov.ph/statistics/spei.asp).
  • POLO (Philippine Overseas Labor Office) verified information from Philippine embassies and consulates on host country labor conditions.

2. Salary Methodology

Government Salaries — SSL VI Tranche 3

All government salary figures on TrabahoWatch are based on the Salary Standardization Law VI (SSL VI), Tranche 3, implemented on January 1, 2026 under Executive Order No. 64 (Series of 2024) signed by President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr.

We display Step 1 (entry-level) salaries for all government positions as this represents what a new appointee actually receives. We do not inflate figures by showing higher step salaries.

Our salary breakdown table (GSIS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG deductions) uses the following mandatory contribution rates:

Deduction Rate Legal Basis
GSIS 9% employee share RA 8291 (GSIS Act)
PhilHealth 2.5% employee share PhilHealth Circular 2023-0014
Pag-IBIG ₱100/month (mandatory maximum) RA 9679 (Pag-IBIG Fund Act)
PERA (not a deduction) +₱2,000/month added EO 611, DBM Circular Letter

Note: The take-home estimate on TrabahoWatch does not include income tax withholding, which varies based on salary level and personal exemptions. Salaries above ₱250,000/year are subject to BIR withholding tax under TRAIN Law (RA 10963).

Private Sector Salaries

Private sector salary ranges are derived from:

  • Employer-published salary ranges in job advertisements
  • JobStreet Philippines salary data (jobstreet.com.ph/en/cms/salary/)
  • Kalibrr salary reports for Philippine tech and professional sectors
  • DOLE Regional Wage Board minimum wage orders (effective 2025–2026, NCR: ₱610/day)

We always clearly label salary figures as "market estimates" when they are ranges and not fixed employer-published amounts.

3. Our Tier System

TrabahoWatch assigns each job listing a Tier rating (1, 2, or 3) based on the depth and completeness of the job guide — not the prestige of the position. A Tier 1 listing simply means we have written a more complete guide with more verified details, not that the job itself is better.

Tier 1 Complete Guide

Full salary breakdown, all requirements listed, step-by-step application guide, all documents listed, official source URL verified, benefits detailed. High-demand positions.

Tier 2 Standard Guide

Core requirements, salary range, and application overview provided. Not all details individually verified — but sourced from credible platforms (JobStreet, employer website).

Tier 3 Essential Info

Basic position details and salary range. May be newly added, pending full verification, or a paused/archived listing kept for reference.

4. Job Status Labels

Every listing on TrabahoWatch carries a status label. Here is what each means:

Open

The vacancy is actively accepting applications. The deadline has not yet passed and the employer has not announced closure of applications.

Closing Soon

The application deadline is within 7 days. Apply immediately. This label appears automatically when the deadline is 7 days away or less.

Paused

The employer has temporarily suspended recruitment for this position. The listing remains visible for informational purposes. Applications may reopen — check the source URL directly.

Archived

The application deadline has passed or the position has been filled. We archive listings (rather than deleting them) because the requirements, salary, and process information remain useful for future reference and for similar upcoming vacancies.

5. Update Cycle and Archive Policy

We review all active job listings on the following schedule:

  • Government jobs: Weekly — we monitor the CSC Job Portal and agency websites for new postings, deadline extensions, and closure announcements.
  • Private sector jobs: Weekly — we check company career pages and JobStreet/Kalibrr for status changes.
  • OFW jobs: Monthly — we verify with the DMW job order database and overseas employer websites. Exchange rates updated quarterly from BSP.
  • Salary data (SSL VI): Updated immediately upon publication of new DBM or EO salary schedules. Next scheduled update: SSL VI Tranche 4 (expected January 1, 2027).
  • CSC exam dates: Updated annually from csc.gov.ph immediately after the CSC releases its examination calendar, typically in November of the preceding year.

Archive Policy: Listings are archived (not deleted) when their deadline passes. Archived listings are clearly marked and excluded from default search results. They remain accessible for research and reference purposes.

6. What TrabahoWatch Does NOT Do

  • We do not accept job applications. We are an information platform, not a recruitment agency. Apply directly to employers using the source URLs we provide.
  • We do not charge fees. TrabahoWatch is completely free. We are not a DMW-licensed recruitment agency and we do not collect placement fees, registration fees, or any payment from job seekers.
  • We do not guarantee employment. Publishing a job listing does not mean we endorse the employer or guarantee the availability of the position. Always verify directly with the employer.
  • We do not act as recruitment intermediary for OFW deployment. All OFW deployment must go through DMW-licensed agencies (dmwph.com). TrabahoWatch only provides educational information.
  • We do not display paid sponsored job listings. No employer or recruitment agency pays TrabahoWatch to feature their vacancies. Our listings are selected based on public availability and relevance to Filipino job seekers.

7. Errors, Updates, and Corrections

We strive for accuracy but acknowledge that job information changes rapidly. If you find an error — incorrect salary, wrong deadline, changed requirements, or a closed position — please let us know:

Report an Inaccuracy

Send us a message through our contact page with the job title, the error, and the correct information with a source link. We aim to update verified corrections within 24 hours on weekdays.
