Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about Philippine government jobs, CSC exams, salary grades, OFW deployment, and private sector employment.

Government Jobs

How do I apply for a government job in the Philippines?

The general process for all Philippine government positions:

  1. Find the vacancy on the CSC Job Portal (csc.gov.ph) or the target agency's website. Most agencies post vacancies 15 days before deadline.
  2. Check your eligibility — you must have CSC Career Service eligibility (Professional or Sub-Professional) or a board passer (RA 1080) license that matches the position's requirement.
  3. Prepare your documents: CSC Form 212 (Personal Data Sheet), TOR, Diploma, board/eligibility certificate (certified true copies), NBI Clearance, and Medical Certificate.
  4. Submit to the agency HRMO before the posted deadline. Most agencies accept walk-in, mail, or email submissions.
  5. Wait for notification. The Selection Board screens applications within 30 days of deadline and notifies shortlisted applicants for assessment or interview.

Can I apply for multiple government jobs at the same time?

Yes. There is no rule prohibiting simultaneous applications to multiple agencies. In fact, applying to multiple vacancies is advisable because government hiring is competitive and can be unpredictable. You can only be appointed to one position at a time — but you can apply to as many as you qualify for. Just make sure you have enough certified true copies of all your documents.

How long does the government hiring process take?

Typically 2–4 months from the application deadline to appointment. Under the CSC rules, agencies must fill vacancies within 4 months of the date of vacancy. However, many agencies take longer in practice. DepEd and DOH recruitment for June (school year start) must begin by January–February. LGU and other agency appointments can happen any time of year.

Do I need to live in the same city/province as the job?

Generally yes — government positions are planted in specific offices and require physical reporting. Some agencies give preference to local residents (especially LGU positions) but this is not a legal requirement. For DepEd, teachers are assigned to specific schools and division offices based on the vacancy. Willingness to be assigned anywhere in the region may increase your chances.

What is the probationary period for government employees?

Under CSC rules, government employees on a permanent appointment serve a 6-month probationary period before regularization. During this period, performance is evaluated under the RPMS (Results-Based Performance Management System). Satisfactory performance leads to permanent status. Government employees cannot be dismissed without cause or due process even during probation.

CSC Eligibility and Exams

What is CSC eligibility and how do I get it?

CSC eligibility is the Civil Service Commission's certification that you have met the qualification standard for government service. There are several ways to obtain CSC eligibility:

  • Civil Service Professional Exam (CSPE): Required for positions SG 15 and above. Register at csc.gov.ph. Passing grade: 80%. Exam held 3 times a year (March, August, November typically).
  • Civil Service Sub-Professional Exam: For positions SG 1–9. Also requires 80% passing score.
  • RA 1080 (Board Passer Eligibility): Automatic eligibility for PRC board exam passers (nurses, teachers, engineers, etc.). No separate CSC exam required.
  • Other special eligibilities: PD 907 (honor graduates), R.A. 7883 (barangay health workers), and other special laws.

How many times can I take the CSC exam?

Under CSC rules, you may take the Civil Service Examination twice a year (one Professional and one Sub-Professional, or twice for the same level with a 3-month interval). There is no lifetime limit on the number of attempts. Many government employees passed after 2–4 attempts. The exam fee is ₱500 per application.

Does CSC eligibility expire?

No. CSC eligibility does not expire. Once you pass the Civil Service Exam, your eligibility is permanent and valid for life for all government positions that require it. Similarly, RA 1080 eligibility (board passer) does not expire even if your PRC license lapses. Your PRC license may expire and need renewal, but your CSC eligibility derived from passing the board exam remains valid permanently.

I am a Licensed Teacher (LET passer). Do I still need to take the CSC exam?

No. As a Licensure Examination for Teachers (LET) passer with a valid PRC Professional Teacher license, you automatically have RA 1080 eligibility — which is equivalent to the CSC Professional examination eligibility. This makes you eligible for Teacher I, Teacher II, and all education-related plantilla positions without taking a separate CSC exam. The same applies to nurses (NLE), engineers, accountants, and all other PRC board exam passers.

Salary Grades and SSL VI

What is SSL VI and how does it differ from SSL V?

The Salary Standardization Law VI (SSL VI) is Republic Act 11466, signed into law in 2020, which revised government salary schedules in 4 tranches:

  • Tranche 1: January 1, 2020
  • Tranche 2: January 1, 2022
  • Tranche 3: January 1, 2026 (currently in effect — EO 64, 2024)
  • Tranche 4: January 1, 2027 (upcoming)

SSL VI significantly raised salaries across all grades. For example, Teacher I increased from ₱22,316 (SSL V) to ₱31,705 (SSL VI Tranche 3) — a 42% increase. All salary figures on TrabahoWatch use SSL VI Tranche 3 rates.

What is the difference between salary grade, step, and tranche?

These three terms describe different dimensions of a government employee's salary:

  • Salary Grade (SG): The position's rank in the government pay scale (SG 1 to SG 33). Determined by the position title and responsibilities. Teacher I = SG 11. Nurse I = SG 15.
  • Step: Within each SG, there are 8 steps (Step 1 to Step 8). New appointees start at Step 1. You move up one step every 3 years of satisfactory service (merit step increment).
  • Tranche: The implementation phase of SSL VI. Each tranche increases the base salary of all SG/step combinations. Tranche 3 (Jan 1, 2026) is the current schedule.

What is PERA and is it included in government salaries?

PERA (Personnel Economic Relief Allowance) is a monthly allowance of ₱2,000 given to all government employees as additional compensation for increased living costs. It is not part of the basic salary (not included in SSL VI salary tables) but is added on top of the basic monthly pay. PERA is also not subject to income tax and is not included in the computation of retirement benefits. TrabahoWatch includes PERA in the gross monthly computation in our salary breakdown tables.

What bonuses do government employees receive?

Government employees receive the following annual bonuses (subject to DBM guidelines and budget availability each year):

  • Mid-Year Bonus: 1 month basic pay, released in May
  • Year-End Bonus: 1 month basic pay, released in November
  • Cash Gift: ₱5,000 per year, released with Year-End Bonus
  • Performance Enhancement Incentive (PEI): ₱5,000–₱25,000 depending on agency performance (not guaranteed every year)
  • Productivity Enhancement Incentive (PEI): Up to ₱25,000 for agencies meeting performance targets

In addition, employees with at least 6 months service receive the bonuses on a pro-rated basis.

OFW Deployment

How do I verify if a recruitment agency is legitimate?

Three steps to verify any recruitment agency:

  1. Visit dmwph.com and use the agency verification search. A valid license number and expiry date should appear.
  2. Call the DMW hotline 1348 (free from PLDT landlines) to verbally confirm.
  3. Visit the agency's physical office and ask to see the DMW license certificate posted on the wall — it is required by law to be displayed prominently.

Any agency without a valid DMW license is illegal. Never pay placement fees to an unverified agency. DMW / POEA Safe Deployment Guide →

What is the maximum placement fee an agency can legally charge?

Under the Migrant Workers Act (RA 10022), the maximum placement fee a licensed recruitment agency can charge is one (1) month of your contracted salary. This fee can only be collected after your visa has been issued and the job order has been verified by DMW. Any fee above one month's salary is illegal. Any fee collected before visa issuance is also illegal. Government-to-government programs (Korea EPS, some Japan programs) charge zero placement fee.

What is DataFlow and why is it required for Saudi Arabia?

DataFlow is a credential verification service (dataflowgroup.com) that Saudi Arabia (and several other Gulf countries) requires for all licensed professionals including nurses, engineers, and pharmacists. It verifies directly with your Philippine university and PRC that your Diploma, TOR, and Board Certificate are authentic. The process takes 6–10 weeks and costs approximately USD 130–200. It is mandatory — no Gulf healthcare employer will process your visa without it. Start DataFlow immediately once you decide to apply for Saudi Arabia — it is the biggest timeline risk in the deployment process.

What is the OEC and do I need it every time I leave?

The OEC (Overseas Employment Certificate) — now called the eOEC — is a document issued by DMW that exempts you from paying travel tax (₱1,620) and terminal fee (₱550) at NAIA. It also serves as your proof of documented status as an OFW.

First-time OFWs: Always need an OEC. Get it at any DMW/OWWA office or online at ofw.gov.ph.

Balikbayan OFWs (returning to same employer, same country, within 1 year): May be exempt — check at ofw.gov.ph.

Changing employer or country: Always need a new OEC.
