What Is the CSC Exam?

The Civil Service Commission Pen and Paper Test (CSC PPT) is the official government eligibility examination in the Philippines. Passing it is a requirement for appointment to most permanent positions in the national government, local government units (LGUs), government-owned and controlled corporations (GOCCs), and state universities and colleges.

The exam is administered twice a year by the Civil Service Commission (CSC) at designated testing centers nationwide. It comes in two levels: the Professional level (for bachelor's degree holders) and the Sub-Professional level (for high school graduates and two-year college completers).

Without CSC eligibility, you cannot be appointed to a permanent government position — no matter how qualified you are. This is why passing the CSC exam is the single most important step for anyone serious about a government career.

Professional vs Sub-Professional

Professional 2nd Level
  • Bachelor's degree graduate or graduating
  • For SG 10 and above positions
  • 170 items — 3 hours and 10 minutes
  • Passing score: 80.00%
  • Exam fee: ₱500
Sub-Professional 1st Level
  • High school graduate or 2nd year college
  • For SG 1–9 positions (clerical, support)
  • 165 items — 3 hours and 5 minutes
  • Passing score: 80.00%
  • Exam fee: ₱500

Requirements and How to Register

Basic Requirements

  • Filipino citizen
  • At least 18 years old on exam day
  • Professional: Bachelor's degree or graduating (last year)
  • Sub-Pro: High school graduate or completed at least 2 years of college
  • Has not taken the same level exam within the last 3 months
  • Has not passed the same level exam before (you only need to pass once — eligibility is permanent)

Documents to Bring on Exam Day

  • Notice of Admission (printed from CSC portal)
  • 2 valid government-issued IDs (passport, PhilSys, UMID, driver's license, voter's ID)
  • 2 recent ID photos (passport size, white background)
  • Black ballpen (at least 2) — pencils are not allowed
  • No cellphones or electronic devices inside

How to Register Online

1

Visit the CSC Online Application System

Go to csonline.csc.gov.ph during the official registration period. Create an account if you don't have one.

2

Fill Out the Application Form

Complete your personal information, choose your exam level (Professional or Sub-Professional), and select your preferred testing center.

3

Pay the ₱500 Exam Fee

Payment can be made at authorized payment centers (7-Eleven, LBC, M Lhuillier, Bayad Center, BancNet) using the payment reference number from the system.

4

Print Your Notice of Admission

After payment confirmation, your Notice of Admission (NOA) will be available in your CSC account. Print it — you must bring it on exam day.

Exam Coverage

The CSC PPT covers five major subject areas for the Professional level, and four areas for Sub-Professional.

Professional Level (170 items)

Subject Area No. of Items Topics Covered
Verbal Ability 40 Vocabulary, grammar, reading comprehension, analogy, English proficiency
Numerical Ability 40 Basic math, fractions, percentages, ratio and proportion, word problems, data interpretation
Reasoning 30 Logical reasoning, abstract reasoning, pattern recognition, series completion
General Information 30 Philippine Constitution, Republic Acts, government structure, current events, geography, history
Filipino (Pagsubok sa Wikang Filipino) 30 Talasalitaan, gramatika, pagbabasa, kasanayan sa Filipino
Total 170 3 hours and 10 minutes total

Passing Score and Results

80.00%

Minimum passing score for both Professional and Sub-Professional levels

Results are released approximately 60 days after the exam date via the CSC website.

Eligibility is permanent — you never have to retake the exam once you pass.

You can verify your eligibility status anytime at csc.gov.ph using your examination number.

Practical Tips to Pass the CSC Exam

Start with Verbal Ability

Verbal Ability has the most items (40) and is heavily weighted in the total score. Build your vocabulary daily with a word-a-day habit. Read English news articles, especially from official government sources.

Memorize the Philippine Constitution

The General Information section always includes questions about the 1987 Constitution. Focus on the Bill of Rights (Article III), citizenship (Article IV), and the general principles (Article II). These are the most frequently tested articles.

Practice Numerical Ability Daily

Speed matters in Numerical Ability. Practice solving percentage, ratio, and word problems without a calculator. The exam is paper-and-pencil — mental math fluency is your most valuable asset here.

Use Official CSC Review Materials

Download the free CSC reviewer from csc.gov.ph. Review CSC Memorandum Circulars on government ethics and civil service rules — these are directly tested in the General Information section.

Time Management on Exam Day

You have approximately 75 seconds per item. Don't get stuck — mark difficult questions and come back to them. Answer all items even if unsure; there is no penalty for wrong answers. Every unanswered item is a definite zero.

Study Filipino (Wikang Filipino)

Many examinees neglect the Filipino section and lose valuable points. The section covers vocabulary (talasalitaan), grammar (gramatika), and reading comprehension — all in Filipino. Read Filipino newspapers like Abante or Pilipino Star Ngayon in the weeks before the exam.

After Passing — What's Next?

1

Download Your Certificate of Eligibility

Log in to your CSC account and download your Certificate of Eligibility (CoE). Keep multiple certified copies — you will need them for every government job application.

2

Browse the CSC Job Portal

Visit csc.gov.ph/career for official government job vacancies. You can also check agency websites directly — DepEd, DOH, LGUs, and others post vacancies on their own portals.

3

Prepare Your PDS (CSC Form 212)

The Personal Data Sheet (PDS) is required for all government job applications. Download the latest version from csc.gov.ph, fill it out carefully and honestly — false declarations are grounds for dismissal and criminal charges.

4

Apply to Specific Agencies

Submit your application directly to the HRMPSB (Human Resource Merit Promotion and Selection Board) of your target agency. Each agency has its own application process and timeline.
