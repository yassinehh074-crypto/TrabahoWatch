Government Salary Guide 2026 Updated

Philippine Government Salary Guide 2026

Effective January 1, 2026 — EO No. 64 (SSL VI Tranche 3) 10 min read

Understanding government compensation in the Philippines goes beyond the basic salary listed in the Salary Grade table. A complete picture includes allowances, bonuses, mandatory deductions, and benefits that together constitute the full annual compensation package for every permanent civil servant.

This guide covers everything you need to know about what a government employee actually earns and receives in 2026, using the updated rates under Executive Order No. 64 (SSL VI), Third Tranche, effective January 1, 2026.

1. Basic Monthly Salary

The basic salary is determined by your Salary Grade (SG) and Step. Under SSL VI Tranche 3 (effective January 1, 2026), the range runs from SG 1 Step 1 (₱14,634/month) to SG 33 (₱449,157/month — the President of the Philippines).

For most career entry positions, the relevant range is:

Position SG Step 1 Basic Salary
Admin Aide III (Clerk) SG 5 ₱16,756
PNP PO1 / BFP FO1 SG 10 ₱29,668
Teacher I SG 11 ₱31,705
Nurse I / NBI Investigator SG 15 ₱42,178
Master Teacher I SG 18 ₱53,818
Division Chief SG 22 ₱81,796

Step increments: Employees advance one step every 3 years of satisfactory performance. Each step is approximately 3% higher than the previous. The maximum is Step 8 — after which salary increases only come through promotion to a higher grade.

Tranche 4 (the final SSL VI increase) is scheduled for January 1, 2027, which will bring another round of salary increases across all grades.

6. GSIS — Your Lifetime Benefits

Beyond your monthly salary, the Government Service Insurance System (GSIS) provides a comprehensive package of benefits that represent significant long-term value to every government employee.

Retirement Benefit

After 15 years of service and at age 60, you receive a monthly pension for life. Longer service = higher pension. The pension is computed based on your average monthly salary in the last 3 years before retirement.

Life Insurance

GSIS provides automatic life insurance coverage. Beneficiaries receive a lump-sum benefit in case of the member's death while in service. Amount is based on the member's salary at time of death.

Housing and Policy Loans

GSIS members can avail of policy loans (up to 3× average monthly salary), emergency loans, and housing loans at preferential interest rates — typically 8% per year compared to commercial rates of 12%+.

7. What to Expect — Tranche 4 (January 1, 2027)

Executive Order No. 64, s. 2024 (SSL VI) provides for four tranches of salary increases. Tranche 3 took effect January 1, 2026. Tranche 4 — the final and largest increase — takes effect January 1, 2027.

Based on the pattern of previous tranches, Tranche 4 is expected to bring increases of approximately 8–15% across most salary grades — with proportionally larger increases at lower grades (SG 1–10) to address the wider compensation gap between entry-level government workers and the private sector.

Once DBM releases the official Tranche 4 table, TrabahoWatch will update all salary figures and the calculator immediately. Monitor dbm.gov.ph for the official announcement.

Already a government employee? Your step increment cycle is unaffected by tranche increases — both the tranche salary increase (January 1, 2027) and your regular step increment (every 3 years) are applied independently, potentially giving you a double salary adjustment if your increment anniversary falls in 2027.
