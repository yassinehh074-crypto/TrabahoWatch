About DOH Healthcare Employment

The Department of Health (DOH) is the principal health agency of the Philippines, overseeing national hospitals, regional medical centers, and community health programs. It operates major facilities including the Philippine General Hospital (PGH), East Avenue Medical Center, Rizal Medical Center, and Batangas Medical Center, among others.

DOH nurses and health workers play a dual role — providing clinical care in hospitals AND participating in national public health programs including immunization campaigns, disease surveillance, and community outreach.

Government hospital nurses also receive night differential pay (for shifts between 10 PM and 6 AM), hazard pay, and uniform allowance — adding meaningfully to the base salary.

How to Apply for DOH Jobs

1

Monitor DOH and CSC Job Portals

Visit doh.gov.ph and the CSC Job Portal for official vacancy postings. DOH regional offices and individual hospitals also post vacancies on their own websites and official Facebook pages.

2

Prepare Your Application Documents

Required documents: CSC Form 212 (PDS) with signature and ID photo, OTR (certified), Diploma, PRC ID and Board Certificate (original + photocopy), NBI Clearance (within 6 months), and Medical Certificate from a DOH-accredited physician.

3

Submit to the DOH Human Resource Division

Submit to the Human Resource Division of the specific DOH hospital or regional office where the vacancy exists. Bring original documents for verification. Some DOH facilities accept online applications — check each facility's announcement for specific instructions.

4

Written Exam and Panel Interview

DOH typically conducts a written nursing or clinical competency exam followed by a structured panel interview with the HRMO and clinical supervisors. Exam coverage includes pharmacology, clinical procedures, and critical thinking scenarios relevant to the specific unit or facility.

Requirements for Nurse I (Most Applied Position)

Required

  • Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN)
  • Active PRC Nursing License (RA 1080) — current and valid
  • Filipino citizen, no pending criminal case
  • Willing to work rotating shifts including nights, weekends, and holidays

Preferred (Higher CAR Score)

  • At least 6 months of clinical experience post-licensure
  • BLS (Basic Life Support) certification — current
  • ACLS certification (strong advantage for ICU/ER positions)
  • NLE rating above 85% (adds points in ranking)
  • Experience in public health programs (immunization, disease surveillance)

Why Choose DOH Nursing?

₱42,178 Starting Salary

DOH Nurse I at SG 15 earns more than most private hospital entry-level nurses. With PERA and night differential, gross monthly pay can reach ₱46,000+.

Foundation for OFW

DOH clinical experience is the most credible foundation for OFW nursing deployment. Saudi MOH, Canadian hospitals, and NHS all value Philippine government hospital experience.

Full GSIS Benefits

As a permanent government employee, you receive GSIS retirement, life insurance, Pag-IBIG housing fund, PhilHealth, and all 13th month and bonus benefits.

Filipino Nurses Abroad — Saudi, Canada, Japan, UK

DOH nursing experience opens doors to the world's top OFW destinations. Saudi Arabia: ₱75,000/month tax-free. Canada: CAD 42/hr + PR pathway.
