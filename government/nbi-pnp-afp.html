PNP / BFP Selection Process

All stages are eliminatory — you must pass each one to advance to the next.

1

Submit Application at Recruitment Office

For PNP: apply at the nearest PNP Regional Office or District Headquarters. For BFP: apply at BFP Regional offices. Bring complete documents including PSA Birth Certificate, Diploma, TOR, NBI Clearance, and passport photos.

2

Written Entrance Examination

Covers general knowledge, reading comprehension, numerical ability, and logical reasoning. For PNP: the NAPOLCOM Entrance Examination. For BFP: the BFP-administered entrance exam. Passing score varies per recruitment cycle — typically 70% or above.

3

Physical Agility Test (PAT)

The most common elimination stage. Components include 100-meter dash, 3km run, push-ups, sit-ups, and sometimes a 25-meter swim (for certain units). Train specifically for these components at least 3 months in advance. Many qualified applicants fail here due to lack of preparation.

4

Medical, Dental, and Neuro-Psychiatric Examination

Comprehensive medical examination including vision, hearing, physical examination, and drug test. The neuro-psychiatric evaluation assesses mental fitness for uniformed service. Be honest — dishonesty in medical declarations is automatic disqualification.

5

Background Investigation (BI)

Thorough investigation of your character, associations, and personal history. Investigators interview neighbors, barangay officials, former employers, and teachers. Any criminal record, pending case, or disqualifying association surfaces here. Honesty in all forms is critical.

6

Final Deliberation Board and Training

Candidates who pass all stages appear before the Deliberation Board. Successful candidates are enrolled in the 6-month Public Safety Basic Recruit Course (PSBRC) for PNP, or the Fire Officer Basic Course (FOBC) for BFP. Training is mandatory before official appointment.

NBI — Special Investigator Application

NBI Special Investigators handle criminal cases involving cybercrime, drug trafficking, human trafficking, and other major offenses. Unlike PNP and BFP, NBI positions are civilian career service positions — not uniformed service — requiring CSC Professional eligibility.

Law graduates (members of the Philippine Bar) are highly preferred for investigator positions, though Criminology, Political Science, and related degrees are also accepted. The background investigation for NBI is extremely thorough — even more rigorous than PNP/BFP.

NBI Investigator Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Criminology, Law, Political Science, or related field
  • CSC Professional Eligibility (2nd Level)
  • Strong analytical, research, and writing skills
  • No criminal record — character investigation is the most rigorous in the government
  • Philippine Bar admission (significantly boosts candidacy for legal investigator roles)

Before You Apply — Key Reminders

Train Early

The Physical Agility Test (PAT) eliminates the most applicants. Start training your 100m dash, 3km run, push-ups, and sit-ups at least 3 months before you apply. Many academically qualified applicants fail the PAT.

No Tattoos on Visible Areas

PNP and BFP strictly prohibit tattoos on the face, neck, and hands. Tattoos on other body parts must not be visible when in uniform. This is an absolute disqualifier — not negotiable during application.

Complete Honesty on All Forms

Background investigators are thorough. Any undisclosed criminal case, disqualifying association, or false declaration — however minor — will surface and result in permanent disqualification from uniformed service.
