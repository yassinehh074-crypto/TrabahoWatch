Government Employee Benefits & Allowances

These are provided on top of basic salary for all permanent government employees.

PERA

Personnel Economic Relief Allowance — ₱2,000/month for all permanent employees.

Mid-Year Bonus

1 month basic salary — released every May. Requires at least 4 months of service in the semester.

Year-End Bonus

1 month basic salary — released every November. Together with the Cash Gift of ₱5,000.

13th Month Pay

Constitutionally mandated. 1 month basic salary released every December.

Clothing Allowance

₱6,000/year — released in the first semester (usually March or April).

PEI

Productivity Enhancement Incentive — ₱5,000/year released every December.

Mandatory Monthly Deductions

GSIS — 9%

Government Service Insurance System. Covers retirement, life insurance, disability, and loans. Government counterpart: 12%.

PhilHealth — 2.5%

National health insurance. Employee share is 2.5% of basic salary (total premium is 5%, split equally).

Pag-IBIG — ₱100

Home Development Mutual Fund. Fixed ₱100/month. Government counterpart: ₱100. Earns dividends and enables housing loans.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between SSL VI Tranche 3 and Tranche 4?

Tranche 3 is currently in effect (January 1, 2026). Tranche 4 — the final tranche of SSL VI — is scheduled to take effect on January 1, 2027. Tranche 4 will bring another round of salary increases for all civilian government personnel.

How often do government employees advance to the next step?

Employees advance one step every 3 years of satisfactory performance. Each step increment is approximately 3% of the Step 1 rate. The maximum is Step 8, after which further salary increases only come through promotion to a higher salary grade.

Is PERA included in the basic salary computation?

No. PERA (₱2,000/month) is a separate allowance not included in the basic salary. It is not subject to GSIS, PhilHealth, or Pag-IBIG deductions, and is not counted in computing retirement benefits. GSIS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG deductions are based on basic salary only.

What is the salary of a Teacher I in 2026?

Under SSL VI Tranche 3, a DepEd Teacher I (SG 11, Step 1) earns a basic monthly salary of ₱31,705. With PERA of ₱2,000, gross monthly pay is ₱33,705. After GSIS (9%), PhilHealth (2.5%), and Pag-IBIG (₱100) deductions, estimated take-home pay is approximately ₱30,059 per month.

Do contractual and job-order employees receive the same salary grade?

No. Salary Grade (SG) applies to permanent and plantilla positions only. Job-order (JO) and contractual employees are paid based on the daily or monthly contract rate agreed upon by the agency. JO workers do not receive GSIS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, or government employee benefits unless separately arranged by the contracting agency.
