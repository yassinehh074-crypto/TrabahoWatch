Finance and Banking in the Philippines

The Philippine banking sector is dominated by universal banks — BDO Unibank, BPI, Metrobank, UnionBank, and RCBC among the largest. Combined, they employ hundreds of thousands of Filipinos in branches, back offices, and corporate roles nationwide.

Entry-level bank positions such as Bank Teller or Junior Analyst require a Business, Accountancy, or Finance degree. CPA licensure significantly boosts salary and career advancement in accounting and audit roles.

Fintech companies — GCash, Maya, Tonik, and UNO Digital — are now major employers of finance and tech hybrid roles, often paying premium salaries above traditional banks.

Key Facts About Finance Careers

CPA = Higher Salary

A CPA license from the PRC adds ₱5,000–₱15,000/month to your base salary at most banks and accounting firms. The CPA board exam is offered twice a year.

Big Bank Benefits

Major banks (BDO, BPI, Metrobank) offer comprehensive HMO coverage, annual performance bonuses, rice allowance, and staff loan privileges at preferential interest rates.

Fintech is Booming

GCash, Maya, and other fintech companies now offer salaries 20–40% above traditional banks for analyst and product roles — with startup equity and flexible work arrangements.
