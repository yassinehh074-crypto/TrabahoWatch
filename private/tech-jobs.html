Tech Industry in the Philippines

The Philippine tech industry has grown rapidly, with Ortigas, BGC, and Makati as the main hubs — and an increasing number of remote-first companies hiring nationwide. Filipino developers are in high demand both locally and internationally.

Entry-level junior developers typically earn ₱25,000–₱40,000/month, with senior developers and full-stack engineers commanding ₱80,000–₱120,000+ in established tech companies and startups.

In-demand skills for 2026: React/Vue.js, Node.js, Python, cloud platforms (AWS/GCP/Azure), and mobile development (React Native, Flutter).

Why Tech in the Philippines?

Remote Work Options

Many tech companies offer fully remote or hybrid setups, allowing you to work from anywhere in the Philippines while earning Metro Manila salaries.

Fastest-Growing Sector

Philippine tech jobs grew 35% in 2025. With strong demand for developers, data scientists, and cloud engineers, salaries are rising year over year.

Global Market Access

Filipino developers frequently work with US, UK, and Australian clients — building international portfolios and earning internationally competitive salaries.
