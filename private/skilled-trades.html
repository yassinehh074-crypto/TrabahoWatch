TESDA NC II — Your Key to Skilled Trade Careers

TESDA (Technical Education and Skills Development Authority) National Certificates (NC) are the official qualification framework for skilled workers in the Philippines. NC II is the most widely required level for employment — both locally and for Gulf OFW deployment.

TESDA training is free or heavily subsidized through Training for Work Scholarship Programs (TWSP). Training centers are nationwide — apply at tesda.gov.ph.

NC II holders in electrical, welding, and plumbing are among the most in-demand OFW workers in Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Qatar — earning AED 1,500–3,500/month tax-free (≈ ₱23,000–₱54,000).

Skilled Trades Career Facts

No College Degree Needed

TESDA NC II requires only high school graduation. Short-course training (2–6 months) is available at TESDA centers nationwide, many with scholarship programs.

High OFW Demand

Gulf countries are always hiring skilled tradesmen. Electricians and welders are among the fastest-deployed OFW categories through DMW-accredited construction and engineering companies.

Infrastructure Boom Locally

The Build Better More program and massive infrastructure projects across the Philippines mean strong local demand for skilled tradesmen through 2028 and beyond.
